Brand Identity

   Senior year, last year, let's see what you've got! For my senior capstone project I had to create my own brand identity. The tangible requirements included a logo or brand style, business cards, envelopes, letterheads, a blog, and a completed resume. The process entailed heavy research, sketching, information architecture, wireframes, coding, weekly critique, cutting and mounting, planning, and printing. The intangible requirement was my internal research. To ask the difficult questions: Who am I as a person? Who am I as a design? What are my strengths? And what are my weaknesses? To name a few. To brand myself successfully I had to dig deeper, and really analyze myself, which was the most difficult type of research.

   As a designer it's hard to settle for a finished product, as there is always room to push, and I am one for not wanting to settle. So it's safe to say, the client for this project was a tough one to please. Even though my brand identity will always be an ongoing project, I feel as though it is successful and I am happy with how it has turned out thus far. So stay tuned for more changes to come, as this is only the beginning!
